## Other · Industry

### Anthropic & Blackstone Launch $1.5B Enterprise AI Services Firm "Ode"
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/anthropic-blackstone-bet-the-next-trillion-dollar-ai-business-is-implementation-not-models/) | [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/enterprise-ai-services-company)

Anthropic partnered with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, Goldman Sachs, and others to launch **Ode with Anthropic**, a $1.5B enterprise AI implementation firm that deploys 100 engineers directly into customer organizations to build bespoke AI systems. Ode operates on a "Claude-first" principle and was seeded by acquiring Fractional AI, a boutique AI services startup that had already been embedded in Blackstone portfolio companies. This marks a notable shift from pure model provision toward high-margin services, signaling that model labs now see *deployment expertise* — not just capability — as the next trillion-dollar category.

## Other · Policy / Infrastructure

### South Korea Unveils $880B National AI Infrastructure Plan
**Source:** [Tech Startups](https://techstartups.com/2026/07/15/top-tech-news-today-july-15-2026-apple-asml-ibm-anthropic-meta-nvidia-openai-stripe-paypal-more/)

South Korean President Lee Jae-myung announced a decade-long AI infrastructure commitment of roughly 1,350 trillion won (~$880B), one of the largest national AI investments ever declared. The plan allocates ~$518B for memory chip manufacturing (Samsung, SK Hynix), ~$550B for AI data centers led by SK Group, GS Group, and Naver, targets 8.4 GW of data center capacity by 2029, and aims to grow South Korea's humanoid robotics market share from 1% to 20% by 2028. The announcement reflects intensifying global competition for AI infrastructure leadership.

## New Agent Tool · Protocol

### MCP 2026 Release Candidate Ships — Stateless Core, MCP Apps & Enterprise Auth
**Source:** [Model Context Protocol Blog](https://blog.modelcontextprotocol.io/posts/2026-07-28-release-candidate/) | [InfoQ](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/07/mcp-ema-enterprise-auth/)

The release candidate for the next major Model Context Protocol specification is now available ahead of the July 28 final freeze. The biggest structural change is a **stateless protocol core** — `tools/call` and similar requests are now self-contained, enabling remote MCP servers to run behind standard load balancers. The spec also introduces **MCP Apps** (servers can ship sandboxed interactive HTML UIs), a redesigned **Tasks extension**, and **Enterprise-Managed Authorisation** that replaces per-server consent prompts with an IdP-backed zero-touch flow. The release represents the largest MCP revision since launch and gives SDK maintainers a ten-week validation window.

## New Model · On-Device AI

### Apple Opens "Siri AI" to Everyone via iOS 27 Public Beta
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/apple-opens-its-new-siri-ai-to-everyone-with-the-ios-27-public-beta/) | [9to5Mac](https://9to5mac.com/2026/07/13/ios-27-public-beta/)

Apple released the iOS 27 public beta on July 13–14, bringing its long-awaited **Siri AI** — rebuilt on Apple Intelligence — to public testers. The new Siri can maintain ongoing conversations, understand on-screen context, take multi-app actions, and tap into personal data on-device. Requests are processed through Apple's on-device Foundation Models (distilled from Google Gemini) combined with Private Cloud Compute for heavier tasks. This is significant because Apple is shipping a genuinely capable, privacy-preserving on-device AI assistant to hundreds of millions of devices, and it signals that frontier AI capability is now normalizing at the OS level.

## Research

### ICML 2026: "Selective Activation Sparsity" Trains Small Models to Rival 3× Larger Ones
**Source:** [Skycrumbs Blog](https://skycrumbs.com/blog/ai-research-july-2026) | [KERSAI](https://kersai.com/ai-breakthroughs-july-2026/)

Researchers at ICML 2026 presented a training method called **selective activation sparsity** that teaches models to activate only the parameters most relevant to each task. Models trained this way performed comparably to models three times their size on reasoning benchmarks, suggesting large efficiency gains for both training and inference. If the approach scales, it could substantially reduce the compute cost of frontier-capable models and enable stronger performance on edge devices.

### FLI 2026 AI Safety Index: Anthropic Scores Highest at C+; Most Labs Fail
**Source:** [AI News Today](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-15-2026) | [Crescendo AI](https://www.crescendo.ai/news/latest-ai-news-and-updates)

The Future of Life Institute released its 2026 AI Safety Index, with Anthropic receiving the highest score at **C+**, followed by OpenAI and Google DeepMind at **C**, Meta at **D+**, and xAI, DeepSeek, and Mistral effectively failing. ## Other · Chips / Hardware

### DeepSeek Announces Custom Inference Silicon to Break Nvidia Dependence
**Source:** [AI Research Breakthroughs — Skycrumbs](https://skycrumbs.com/blog/ai-research-july-2026)

Chinese AI lab DeepSeek announced it is designing proprietary inference silicon to reduce dependence on Nvidia GPUs and Huawei Ascend chips, with a second-generation chip expected before end of 2026. The move mirrors similar efforts by Google (TPUs), Amazon (Trainium), and Anthropic's reported talks with Samsung for custom Claude inference chips. Custom silicon gives labs greater cost control and supply-chain independence — a strategic priority following repeated U.S. export controls on high-end GPUs to China.
