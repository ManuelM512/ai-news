## New Model

### 1. Inkling — Thinking Machines Releases First Open-Weight Multimodal Model
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/thinking-machines-amps-up-its-bet-against-one-size-fits-all-ai-with-its-first-open-model-inkling/) · [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/15/mira-murati-thinking-machines-open-weight-model-inkling) · [Thinking Machines Lab](https://thinkingmachines.ai/news/introducing-inkling/) · [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/inkling/)

Mira Murati's **Thinking Machines Lab** shipped **Inkling** on July 15, 2026 — its first in-house model and the company's opening bid for the open-weight market. Inkling is a 975B-total-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (41B active per token) trained on 45 trillion tokens of text, image, audio, and video, enabling native multimodal reasoning across all four. Full weights are available on Hugging Face under Apache 2.0; Thinking Machines is positioning Inkling less as a finished product and more as a fine-tuning starting point via its **Tinker** customization platform. The lab is valued at $12B.

**Why it matters:** Inkling is the first frontier-style open-weight model to ship natively quadri-modal (text + image + audio + video), and its Apache 2.0 licence removes the usual commercial restrictions. Thinking Machines' "we're not the best, tune us" positioning is a strategic bet that the market will reward customizability over raw benchmark scores — a direct challenge to closed-model incumbents on total cost of ownership.

### 2. Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Still Absent; Stopgap Gemini 3.6 Flash in Partner Testing
**Source:** [9to5Google](https://9to5google.com/2026/07/16/gemini-3-5-pro-delays/) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/google-gemini-launch-delayed-as-tech-falls-short-of-internal-goals) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320736/20260716/rebuilt-gemini-35-pro-misses-third-deadline-google-eyes-stopgap-release.htm) · [Windows Forum](https://windowsforum.com/threads/gemini-3-5-pro-remains-unshipped-july-18-use-flash-now.439120/)

As of July 18, **Gemini 3.5 Pro** remains unshipped — missing its third consecutive internal deadline after a full ground-up rebuild failed to fix structural failures in recursive tool-calling and SVG generation. Alphabet shares fell ~4% on the news. Google is now testing a stopgap, **Gemini 3.6 Flash**, with select partners while the flagship model is further stabilised; no public availability date, pricing, or benchmark disclosure has been set.

**Why it matters:** Gemini 3.5 Pro was supposed to anchor Google's response to GPT-5.6 Sol and Claude Fable 5, both of which have been broadly available for weeks. A stopgap Flash release buys time but signals that the architectural rebuild is deeper than Google anticipated, raising questions about its frontier competitiveness in H2 2026.

## New Agent Tool

### 3. Microsoft Project Perception — Multi-Model AI Cybersecurity Platform
**Source:** [TechRepublic](https://www.techrepublic.com/article/news-microsoft-project-perception-ai-security-tool/) · [NewsBytesApp](https://www.newsbytesapp.com/news/science/microsoft-to-launch-project-perception-ai-cybersecurity-platform-this-month/tldr) · [Windows News](https://windowsnews.ai/article/microsofts-project-perception-aims-to-make-ai-vulnerability-fixing-cheap-enough-to-run-nonstop.439207)

Microsoft is preparing to launch **Project Perception**, an AI-powered vulnerability-detection and remediation platform that routes security tasks across models from Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic based on job complexity and cost. The platform scans code, cloud infrastructure, and endpoints; explains exploitable weaknesses; and proposes concrete fixes — competing directly with Anthropic's **Mythos** cybersecurity offering at a significantly lower expected price point. A private preview of the related **MDASH** vulnerability-scan CLI is already live inside Microsoft Defender.

**Why it matters:** Multi-model routing for security tooling is a new architectural pattern: rather than betting on a single frontier model, Project Perception treats the model layer as interchangeable infrastructure and competes on cost and coverage. If successful, this approach could commoditise the "best model for security tasks" question and shift competition to the orchestration and data-integration layer.

## Other

### 4. Xi Jinping Launches WAICO — 29-Nation Global AI Governance Body
**Source:** [Xinhua](https://english.news.cn/20260717/ce32e833ab5d47f883ad44e1f73cb634/c.html) · [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/17/chinas-xi-jinping-launches-new-ai-alliance-what-is-it) · [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/07/17/nx-s1-5897285/chinas-xi-calls-for-step-up-of-global-effort-in-ai-as-us-curbs-squeeze-chinas-tech-access) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320812/20260717/china-launches-rival-ai-governance-bloc-waic-2026-opens-300-product-debuts.htm)

In his keynote at the **2026 World AI Conference (WAIC)** in Shanghai, President Xi Jinping announced the **World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO)** — a new intergovernmental body headquartered in Shanghai with 29 founding members including Indonesia, Brazil, Malaysia, South Africa, Russia, Pakistan, and Kazakhstan. Xi framed it as an answer to Global South demands for a larger voice in AI governance and pledged 5,000 AI training opportunities for developing countries over five years. The body is explicitly designed to counter Washington-centred AI governance frameworks.

**Why it matters:** WAICO is the most significant institutional challenge to US-dominated AI governance since the EU AI Act. A 29-nation bloc anchored in the Global South, with China's open-source models positioned as a "public good," creates a parallel governance track that could fragment how frontier AI is regulated, deployed, and exported globally.

### 5. Apple Overtakes Nvidia as World's Most Valuable Company (~$5T)
**Source:** [Build Fast with AI](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-18-2026) · [AIToolsRecap](https://aitoolsrecap.com/Blog/AINewsJuly2026.aspx)

As of July 17, **Apple** edged past **Nvidia** to become the world's most valuable publicly traded company, approaching a **$5 trillion market capitalisation**, with Nvidia slipping to second. The shift reflects investors rewarding AI distribution and ecosystem breadth — Apple's on-device AI rollout and enterprise services — alongside raw chip supply.

**Why it matters:** For most of 2025 and early 2026, Nvidia's dominance of the AI chip market drove its valuation lead. Apple's overtaking signals a possible inflection: markets may be starting to price downstream AI distribution and application layers as highly as semiconductor infrastructure, a shift with significant implications for where AI investment flows next.

## Research

### 7. H1 2026 AI Paper Surge: 170,927 Papers Reveal Biggest Shifts in Research Focus
**Source:** [Medium / AI Papers Academy](https://medium.com/@aipapers/170-927-ai-papers-reveal-the-biggest-research-shifts-of-the-first-half-of-2026-f26f3b792824) · [arXiv benchmarking review](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.19749)

An analysis of arXiv ML-category submissions covering the first half of 2026 found **170,927 AI papers** — a pace that, if sustained, would more than double 2024's annual output. The biggest thematic shifts: a surge in agentic-system papers (autonomous reasoning, tool use, multi-agent coordination), a parallel rise in AI-for-Science (drug discovery, materials), and a growing body of work scrutinising AI peer review itself, including papers showing that presentation-only revisions can game automated review systems.

**Why it matters:** The sheer volume signals that AI research is now a mass-participation field with thousands of groups globally contributing simultaneously. The meta-research layer — papers about how AI reviews papers — points to a coming credibility challenge: as AI agents write, submit, and review research, the field may need new integrity infrastructure before the end of 2026.
