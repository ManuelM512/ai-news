## New Model

### 1. Moonshot AI Releases Kimi K3 — Largest Open-Source Model Ever
**Source:** [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/chinas-moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k3-the-largest-open-source-model-ever-rivaling-top-u-s-systems) · [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/16/moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k3-a-2-8-trillion-parameter-open-moe-model-with-kimi-delta-attention-and-1m-context/) · [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/china-s-powerful-new-moonshot-ai-model-closes-gap-with-us-rivals)

China's Moonshot AI launched **Kimi K3** on July 16, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open-weight Mixture-of-Experts model with a custom Kimi Delta Attention architecture and a 1-million-token context window. Two variants shipped: K3 Max (chat and agent workloads) and K3 Swarm Max (large-scale parallel processing). The API is live now; full open weights are promised by July 27.

**Why it matters:** K3 is the largest open-source model ever released, and early benchmarks show it competing neck-and-neck with proprietary frontier systems from OpenAI and Anthropic. A 2.8T open-weight model with 1M context could shift where developers set the open-source baseline for 2026's second half.

### 2. Google Gemini 3.5 Pro Misses Third Launch Deadline
**Source:** [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320736/20260716/rebuilt-gemini-35-pro-misses-third-deadline-google-eyes-stopgap-release.htm) · [HackerNoon](https://hackernoon.com/google-delays-gemini-35-pro-to-july-17-the-strategic-play-behind-the-scrapped-base-model)

Google DeepMind's **Gemini 3.5 Pro** has missed three consecutive internal deadlines after engineers scrapped the original base model and restarted pre-training. The rebuilt model reportedly still exhibits frequent hallucinations and falls short of GPT-5.6 Sol on key benchmarks. Google is now evaluating a stopgap release while the flagship model stabilizes.

**Why it matters:** Gemini 3.5 Pro was expected to anchor Google's competitive response to the GPT-5.6 family and Apple's on-device partnership. Repeated delays at this scale signal deeper engineering challenges with the architectural rebuild and raise questions about Google's near-term frontier positioning.

### 3. OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Family: Sol, Terra, and Luna
**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/) · [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/09/openai-releases-gpt-5-6-a-three-tier-model-family-with-programmatic-tool-calling/) · [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/gpt-5-6/)

OpenAI publicly released **GPT-5.6** on July 9 as a three-tier family: **Sol** (flagship, $5/$30 per 1M tokens), **Terra** (balanced, $2.50/$15), and **Luna** (fast/cheap, $1/$6). All three share a 1M-token context window, a February 2026 knowledge cutoff, and 128K max output tokens. The release also introduced **ChatGPT Work**, an autonomous agent that carries out multi-step job-level tasks rather than single-turn answers. Sol is 54% more token-efficient on agentic coding tasks than its predecessor.

**Why it matters:** The three-tier pricing structure sets a new competitive floor: Luna undercuts many mid-tier models while Sol extends the frontier. ChatGPT Work represents OpenAI's most direct push into autonomous workplace agents, competing directly with enterprise automation platforms.

## Research

### 7. Gartner: 40% of Enterprise Apps to Embed Agents by End of 2026
**Source:** [Technology Radar July 2026](https://www.hectorpincheira.com/en/news/technological-radar-july-2026-ai-agents-go-into-production-and-governance-doesnt-keep-up/) · [AI Agent Store](https://aiagentstore.ai/ai-agent-news/this-week)

Gartner's July 2026 Technology Radar projects that **40% of enterprise applications will have embedded AI agents by the end of 2026**, up from less than 5% in 2025. The report notes that production deployment is accelerating far faster than governance frameworks can follow, flagging identity, auditability, and cost containment as the three critical unresolved issues.

**Why it matters:** A 35-percentage-point jump in one year is extraordinary adoption velocity for any enterprise technology category. The governance gap highlighted in the report is likely to drive regulatory attention and new tooling needs around agent observability and access control.

### 8. ITU Launches Focus Group on Trust and Identity for Agentic AI
**Source:** [Agentic.ai](https://agentic.ai/news)

The International Telecommunication Union (ITU) announced a new **Focus Group on Trust and Identity for Humans and Agentic AI** at the AI for Good Global Summit. The group aims to build international frameworks for digital identity, accountability, and behavioral standards across the agent lifecycle — spanning how agents authenticate, how their actions are attributed, and how they are audited.

**Why it matters:** As AI agents act autonomously on behalf of humans and organizations, the question of identity and accountability becomes critical infrastructure. An ITU-level standardization effort signals that the international standards community views agent identity as a foundational problem, not a niche concern.
