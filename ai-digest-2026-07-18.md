# AI News Digest — 2026-07-18

## New Model

### 1. Moonshot AI Releases Kimi K3 — 2.8T MoE Model with 1M Context
**Source:** [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/16/moonshot-ai-releases-kimi-k3-a-2-8-trillion-parameter-open-moe-model-with-kimi-delta-attention-and-1m-context/) | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-17/china-s-powerful-new-moonshot-ai-model-closes-gap-with-us-rivals) | [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/)

Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (896 experts, 16 activated per token) with a novel Kimi Delta Attention (KDA) hybrid linear-attention architecture and native vision understanding supporting up to a 1 million token context window. It debuted at No. 3 on the Artificial Analysis AI leaderboard — behind Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol — and outperformed both on Arena.ai's front-end web development benchmark, priced at $3/M input and $15/M output tokens, with open weights planned for release by July 27.

**Why it matters:** The largest open-weight model release to date from a Chinese lab closes the gap with US frontier models and applies additional pressure on pricing across the top tier; the forthcoming open-weight release will be significant for the research community.

### 2. GPT-5.6 Family Now Broadly Available
**Source:** [Nextgov/FCW](https://www.nextgov.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/07/openais-advanced-gpt-56-models-be-available-public/414651/) | [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/09/ai-openai-gpt-release)

OpenAI completed the broad rollout of GPT-5.6 — originally staggered at the U.S. government's request — with three variants: Sol (maximum capability), Terra (balanced), and Luna (speed-optimised). The suite targets different latency and cost trade-offs across enterprise and consumer workloads.

**Why it matters:** Full public access removes the queue bottleneck that had limited evaluation and adoption; the tiered naming convention signals OpenAI is shifting to a product-line model rather than single flagship releases.

## New Agent Tool

### 3. Perplexity Launches SPACE — Secure Sandboxes for Long-Running Agents
**Source:** [Perplexity Blog](https://www.perplexity.ai/hub/blog/secure-sandboxes-for-agents) | [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/07/15/perplexity-launches-secure-sandbox-make-ai-agents-secure-powerful/) | [AlphaSignal](https://alphasignal.ai/news/perplexity-s-space-runs-secure-long-running-agents-5x-faster)

Perplexity released SPACE (Sandboxed Platform for Agentic Code Execution), the runtime layer behind its Computer agent. SPACE provides isolated execution environments, out-of-band credential injection (credentials never enter the sandbox), and session persistence so long-horizon tasks can be paused, resumed, or branched. The switch cut median sandbox creation latency from 185ms to 60ms (3.1×) and P90 from 447ms to 89ms; 100% of Computer sessions now run on SPACE.

**Why it matters:** Security and reliability at the infrastructure layer is an unsolved bottleneck for agentic deployment; SPACE's architecture — especially secrets management outside the sandbox boundary — is an early production-grade reference design for the industry.

## Research

### 6. Selective Activation Sparsity — Models That Match 3× Their Size
**Source:** [Skycrumbs / NeurIPS 2026 submission](https://skycrumbs.com/blog/ai-research-july-2026)

Researchers submitted to NeurIPS 2026 a training method called "selective activation sparsity," which teaches models to activate only the most task-relevant parameters on a per-input basis. Models trained with this approach matched the reasoning benchmark scores of models approximately three times their parameter count, at a fraction of training and inference cost.

**Why it matters:** If the results hold up to peer review, this technique could significantly reduce the cost of deploying capable models on constrained hardware (phones, edge devices) and compress the compute gap between labs with and without massive data centres.

### 7. WAIC 2026 Showcases AI-for-Science Leap from Assisted to Autonomous
**Source:** [36Kr / WAIC 2026](https://eu.36kr.com/en/p/3884022753882115)

The World Artificial Intelligence Conference (WAIC 2026, July 17–20, Shanghai) opened with a focus on AI for Science (AI4S), highlighting a shift from "assisted computing" — AI accelerating human-designed experiments — to "autonomous discovery," where AI systems propose and execute entire research cycles independently. Demonstrations spanned drug discovery, materials science, and climate modelling.

**Why it matters:** Autonomous scientific discovery is one of the highest-value potential applications of frontier AI; WAIC 2026's AI4S track signals that Chinese labs and institutions are investing heavily in this direction and treating it as a national priority.

### 8. ICML 2026 Efficiency Papers Highlight Sub-Parameter Scaling
**Source:** [Skycrumbs](https://skycrumbs.com/blog/ai-research-july-2026)

Several papers at ICML 2026 this week demonstrated that targeted efficiency techniques — including pruning, quantisation-aware distillation, and sparse attention variants — can deliver comparable performance to much larger baseline models. The results suggest "sub-parameter scaling" (extracting more capability per active parameter) may become as important a research axis as raw scale.

**Why it matters:** As the cost of training frontier models approaches the limits of available capital and power, efficiency research that decouples capability from parameter count could fundamentally alter which organisations can compete at the frontier.
