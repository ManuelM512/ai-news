### 1. China's AI Companion Regulations Hit: Alibaba Qwen Goes Dark Today, ByteDance Follows July 15
**Source:** [AI News](https://www.artificialintelligence-news.com/news/china-ai-companion-rules/) · [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/319703/20260704/china-ai-companion-law-arrives-july-15-doubao-qwen-agent-data-will-deleted.htm) · [Quartz](https://qz.com/bytedance-alibaba-ai-companion-chatbot-china-rules-070626)

China's **Interim Measures for the Administration of AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services** take effect July 15, and the industry is already complying ahead of schedule. Alibaba's **Qwen** shut down its humanlike and user-created agent features on July 10; ByteDance's **Doubao** will disable its agent functions on July 15. The regulation — co-issued in April by five Chinese agencies including the CAC — bans virtual companion and family-member services for minors, requires security assessments for services crossing 1M registered users or 100K monthly actives, and mandates guardian consent for users under 14. Work assistants and educational bots are explicitly exempted.

**Why it matters:** This is China's first comprehensive regulatory framework for emotionally-engaged AI companions, and it's already forcing two of China's largest AI product companies to pull features today. It draws a clear policy line between work-oriented agents (permitted) and emotional-companionship AI (regulated), a distinction that will likely influence regulatory debates globally.

## Research

### 2. Google Releases First AI Mode Usage Data: 1 Billion Monthly Users, Search Behavior Fundamentally Changed
**Source:** [Search Engine Journal](https://www.searchenginejournal.com/google-shares-first-ai-mode-usage-data-after-one-year/575443/) · [WrittenlyHub](https://www.writtenlyhub.com/news/google-ai-mode-1-billion-users-adoption-data) · [Thrive Agency](https://thriveagency.com/news/google-ai-mode-statistics-search-behavior-data/)

Google published the first comprehensive usage data for **AI Mode** — now one year since launch — showing it has surpassed **1 billion monthly active users** globally, making it the fastest-growing Google product to reach that milestone. Key behavioral findings: the average AI Mode query is 7.2 words vs. 4.0 for traditional search; follow-up queries grew 40%+ per month in the U.S.; more than one in six searches is now multimodal (voice or image); and queries have more than doubled every quarter. A parallel Semrush study finds 93% of AI Mode sessions end without a click to an external site.

**Why it matters:** These numbers confirm that AI Mode is reshaping search behavior at a scale few anticipated in just twelve months. The 93% zero-click rate has profound implications for publishers and SEO, while the query-length and multimodal growth signal that users are adapting to a fundamentally different search paradigm.

## New Model

### 3. NVIDIA Releases Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower: Open-Weight Diffusion LM with 2.42× Throughput
**Source:** [TechTimes](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320121/20260710/diffusion-llm-learns-its-own-draft-model-nvidia-releases-tri-mode-open-weights.htm) · [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/01/nvidia-releases-nemotron-labs-twotower/)

NVIDIA released **Nemotron-Labs-TwoTower** open weights on Hugging Face under a commercial license, the latest in its Nemotron-Labs diffusion series. The model family (3B, 8B, and 14B) operates in a tri-mode architecture — autoregressive, diffusion, and self-speculation decoding — allowing the same checkpoint to serve as both draft and verification model in speculative decoding, eliminating the need for a separate draft model. The 8B instruct variant achieves **2.42× higher throughput** versus baseline AR models while retaining 98.7% of output quality, and delivers 6× more accepted tokens per forward pass than Eagle3.

**Why it matters:** Eliminating the separate draft model is a meaningful operational simplification for teams running speculative decoding at scale. Open weights under a commercial license make this accessible for self-hosted inference pipelines, and the throughput gains are real and substantial.

### 4. Anthropic's Claude Goes GA on Microsoft Azure Powered by NVIDIA GB300 Blackwell Ultra
**Source:** [NVIDIA Blog](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/anthropic-nvidia-gb300-blackwell-ultra-microsoft-azure/) · [WCCFTech](https://wccftech.com/nvidia-blackwell-ultra-powers-anthropic-claude-models-in-microsoft-azure/) · [Silicon Report](https://siliconreport.com/anthropics-claude-models-now-run-on-azure-with-nvidia-blackwell-backed-by-30-billion-compu-b532256f)

Anthropic's **Claude Opus 4.8** and **Claude Haiku 4** are now generally available in **Microsoft Azure AI Foundry**, running on NVIDIA GB300 NVL72 systems with Quantum-X800 InfiniBand networking. The GB300 hardware delivers ~40% faster token generation compared to H100 nodes for Claude Sonnet workloads. The release is backed by a broader $30B compute commitment between Microsoft, NVIDIA, and Anthropic. This marks Anthropic's first deployment on NVIDIA hardware at this scale and its first GA offering on a hyperscaler platform.

**Why it matters:** Azure availability dramatically expands Claude's enterprise distribution reach, and GB300-class performance makes long-horizon agentic workloads meaningfully cheaper per token. Enterprise teams already invested in Azure now have a first-party path to Anthropic models without leaving their cloud provider.

Several agencies are already live: the Department of Motor Vehicles is using Claude for customer service, the Department of Health Care Services for Medicaid administration workflows, and the California Governor's Office of Emergency Services for cybersecurity triage.

### 7. Etched Emerges from Stealth: $800M Raised, $1B in Contracts for Transformer-Dedicated Inference Chip
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/nvidia-competitor-etched-hits-5b-valuation-1b-in-sales-for-ai-chip/) · [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/30/inference-chip-startup-etched-launches-800m-funding/) · [GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/30/3319922/0/en/etched-emerges-from-stealth-with-working-chip-800m-raised-and-over-1b-in-customer-contracts.html)

**Etched** came out of stealth with a working chip, $800M raised across multiple rounds, and over $1B in signed customer contracts — now at a **$5B post-money valuation**. Its chip, **Sohu**, is purpose-built exclusively for transformer inference, stripping out the generality required for training or other tasks to maximize throughput for the specific workload AI deployments actually run most of the time. First racks are shipping this summer. The 400-person team draws primarily from NVIDIA, Broadcom, Google TPU, and SK Hynix.

**Why it matters:** NVIDIA still dominates AI infrastructure, but Sohu's hyper-specialization represents a structural bet that inference is so dominant in deployed AI workloads that sacrificing training versatility for inference-only speed is commercially viable. With $1B already in contracts before shipping a single rack, there's clear enterprise demand for alternatives to general-purpose GPU inference.
