## New Model

### 1. OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra & Luna
**Source:** [OpenAI Blog](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/) · [Neowin](https://www.neowin.net/news/openai-to-release-gpt-56-sol-terra-and-luna-on-july-9/)

OpenAI publicly released the GPT-5.6 family on July 9, 2026: **Sol** (flagship, deepest reasoning), **Terra** (balanced performance at 2× lower cost than GPT-5.5), and **Luna** (fast and lowest-cost). Sol introduces a new "ultra mode" that orchestrates subagents internally to accelerate long-horizon tasks. The release follows a limited preview gated behind a U.S. government safety review that cleared faster than expected.

**Why it matters:** This is the most significant OpenAI model release of 2026. Ultra mode marks OpenAI's first productized multi-agent architecture baked directly into a model, and the tiered Sol/Terra/Luna lineup aims to cover every price point from consumer to enterprise.

### 2. OpenAI Releases GPT-Live Full-Duplex Voice Models
**Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/08/openai-releases-new-voice-models-for-more-natural-live-conversations/) · [VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/openai-launches-gpt-live-a-full-duplex-voice-upgrade-that-lets-chatgpt-talk-more-like-a-person)

OpenAI launched **GPT-Live-1** and **GPT-Live-1 mini** on July 8, replacing Advanced Voice Mode in ChatGPT. Built on a true full-duplex architecture, the models can listen and speak simultaneously, handle natural turn-taking, and delegate complex queries to a background frontier model mid-conversation. Rollout is live on iOS, Android, and web.

**Why it matters:** Full-duplex voice that delegates to stronger reasoning models mid-conversation brings ChatGPT Voice notably closer to a real-time assistant rather than a walkie-talkie interface.

### 3. xAI Releases Grok 4.5 — Coding-Focused at Opus-Class Performance
**Source:** [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-grok-new-model) · [xAI Announcement](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5)

xAI shipped **Grok 4.5** on July 8, built on the 1.5T-parameter V9 foundation and trained on real Cursor developer session data. It ranks 4th on the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index, lands on par with GPT-5.5 on coding benchmarks, and costs $2/$6 per million tokens — over 60% cheaper than Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5. The 500k context window is a reduction from Grok 4.3's 1M. EU availability is targeted for mid-July.

**Why it matters:** Grok 4.5 credibly enters the top-tier coding/agentic tier at a price point that undercuts all peers, directly pressuring OpenAI Codex and Anthropic's agent offerings.

## New Agent Tool

### 4. Anthropic Launches Claude Cowork for Mobile
**Source:** [AI News Today — July 10](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-10-2026)

Anthropic launched **Claude Cowork** on iOS and Android on July 9 — the same day OpenAI released ChatGPT Work — enabling users to delegate multi-step knowledge-work tasks and monitor long-running agentic sessions from mobile. Previously, Claude's agentic sessions required an active desktop connection to persist.

**Why it matters:** Removing the desktop constraint is a significant UX unlock for enterprise agentic workflows; users can now hand off complex tasks from their phone and check back on progress remotely.

### 5. OpenAI Merges Codex + ChatGPT into a Single Super App
**Source:** [AI News Today — July 10](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-10-2026)

OpenAI merged its **Codex** developer coding agent and **ChatGPT** into a unified product called **ChatGPT Work**, launched July 9. The consolidation means coding tasks, agentic workflows, and conversational AI share a single interface and context, rather than requiring users to switch between separate products.

## New Model

### 6. Z.ai's GLM-5.2 Tops Open-Weight Rankings — Built Entirely on Huawei Silicon
**Source:** [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2026/07/03/what-is-glm-52-the-new-chinese-ai-model-thats-rivalling-anthropic) · [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/z-ai-free-glm-5-2-tops-the-open-weight-ai-rankings-on-all-huawei-silicon)

**GLM-5.2**, released by China's Z.ai (Zhipu AI) on July 1, is an open-weight model that trails Anthropic's Opus 4.8 by just 1% on long-horizon engineering benchmarks while undercutting it dramatically on price ($1.40/$4.40 vs. $5/$25 per million tokens). It was trained entirely on ~100,000 Huawei Ascend 910B chips using MindSpore — no Nvidia silicon. The model supports a 1M token context window.

**Why it matters:** GLM-5.2 demonstrates that frontier-class coding performance is now achievable without U.S. hardware or closed proprietary training stacks, intensifying the geopolitical dimension of the AI race.

## Research

### 7. ResearcherBench: New Benchmark for Evaluating Deep AI Research Systems
**Source:** [arXiv — ResearcherBench](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2507.16280)

A new paper introduces **ResearcherBench**, a benchmark designed to evaluate AI systems on scientific research tasks at the frontier of human knowledge — not standard Q&A, but genuine open-ended inquiry. It distinguishes between retrieval-augmented lookup and actual reasoning over ambiguous or novel scientific questions.

**Why it matters:** As AI is increasingly deployed for drug discovery, materials science, and climate modeling, better evaluation frameworks for research-grade reasoning are critical for understanding where these systems actually stand.
