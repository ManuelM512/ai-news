## New Model

### OpenAI Launches GPT-5.6: Sol, Terra, and Luna
**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/previewing-gpt-5-6-sol/) · [Engadget](https://www.engadget.com/2210308/openai-rolls-out-gpt5-6-july-9/) · [Dataconomy](https://dataconomy.com/2026/07/08/openai-gpt-5-6-launch-july-9/)

OpenAI publicly launched its GPT-5.6 family today — Sol (flagship, $5/$30 per M tokens), Terra (balanced, $2.50/$15), and Luna (budget, $1/$6) — after the Trump administration granted global release approval following a mandatory 30-day government review under a June cybersecurity executive order. Sol launches on Cerebras wafer-scale hardware for select customers, reaching up to 750 tokens/second — roughly 15× current GPU inference speeds.

**Why it matters:** This is the first time a frontier AI model required U.S. government sign-off before public release, setting a precedent for regulation of the most capable models. Sol's Cerebras deployment also pushes real-time agentic applications to a new performance ceiling.

### xAI Releases Grok 4.5
**Source:** [xAI](https://x.ai/news/grok-4-5) · [Axios](https://www.axios.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-grok-new-model) · [MarkTechPost](https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/07/08/spacexai-releases-grok-4-5/)

SpaceXAI released Grok 4.5 publicly on July 8, built on a 1.5-trillion-parameter V9 architecture and co-trained with Cursor (acquired by SpaceX for $60B in June). Priced at $2/$6 per M tokens and running at ~80 tokens/second, it claims Opus-class quality at significantly lower cost and token usage — resolving SWE-Bench tasks in an average 15,954 output tokens vs. 67,020 for Opus 4.8.

**Why it matters:** Grok 4.5's 4.2× token-efficiency advantage over Opus 4.8 on coding benchmarks, combined with deep Cursor integration, positions it as a serious contender for agentic coding workloads at a fraction of the price of comparable frontier models.

### Meta Launches Muse Image — First In-House AI Image Generator
**Source:** [Meta Newsroom](https://about.fb.com/news/2026/07/introducing-muse-image-meta-ai/) · [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/07/meta-rolls-out-muse-a-new-ai-image-generator/) · [The Next Web](https://thenextweb.com/news/meta-muse-image-ai-generation-instagram-whatsapp)

Meta's Superintelligence Labs (led by Alexandr Wang) released Muse Image on July 7, integrated into Meta AI across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. The model pairs with a planning layer (Muse Spark) that thinks through prompts, fetches real-time web context, and blends multiple visual references before generating. It supports 30+ new Instagram Story effects and conversational prompt input.

**Why it matters:** Meta is the last major platform company to ship its own image-generation model, and Muse Image's deep social-product embedding gives it massive distribution overnight. The launch has already drawn privacy criticism over a feature that allows manipulation of public Instagram users' photos.

## New Framework

### Microsoft Agent Framework 1.13.0 — Expanded Skills APIs and Hosted Agents GA
**Source:** [Microsoft Agent Framework Blog](https://devblogs.microsoft.com/agent-framework/microsoft-agent-framework-at-build-2026-announce/) · [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/microsoft/agent-framework/releases)

Microsoft shipped Agent Framework (MAF) 1.13.0 in July, adding expanded skills APIs, new file-editing tools, richer approval and caching options, and improved session isolation in Foundry Hosting. Hosted Agents — sandboxed sessions with dedicated compute, memory, and filesystem — reached general availability in early July. The framework is runtime-agnostic, supporting MAF, GitHub Copilot SDK, and LangGraph agents without rewrites.

**Why it matters:** MAF 1.0 (GA since April 2026) unified AutoGen and Semantic Kernel into one platform; the 1.13.0 skills-API expansion and hosted sandboxes make it significantly more capable for production multi-agent deployments without infrastructure overhead.

## Research

### Institutional Red-Teaming: Deployment Rules Shape Multi-Agent Safety
**Source:** [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent)

Yujiao Chen's paper "Institutional Red-Teaming: Deployment Rules, Not Just Models, Causally Shape Multi-Agent AI Safety" (submitted July 9) argues that safety in multi-agent systems depends critically on the governance rules under which agents operate — not just model-level alignment — and proposes a structured red-teaming methodology targeting institutional constraints.

**Why it matters:** As multi-agent systems become production infrastructure, this work reframes safety evaluation away from model behavior alone, suggesting that policy design and deployment rules are first-class safety levers deserving systematic adversarial testing.
